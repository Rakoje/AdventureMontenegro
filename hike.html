National park Durmitor

Explore Durmitor

Durmitor National Park has been on the UNESCO list of World Natural and Cultural Heritage since 1980. The National Park includes the Durmitor mountain massif, the canyons of the Tara, Draga, Sušice rivers and the highest part of the Komarnica canyon valley, from which numerous mountain peaks rise, 48 of which are over 2,000 meters above sea level.

The highest peak is Bobot's hip, 2,525 meters above sea level.
The area of the Park is 34,500 ha, and the territory belongs to the following municipalities: Žabljak, Šavnik, Plužine, Pljevlja and Mojkovac.
Durmitor is adorned with 18 glacial lakes called mountain eyes. The largest and most famous is the Black Lake.

The climate is temperate-continental, sub-mountainous and mountainous. It is characterized by long and cold winters and relatively short and cool summers. The transitional period between the seasons is less pronounced, and autumn is slightly warmer than spring.
